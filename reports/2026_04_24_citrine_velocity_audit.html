Methodology Notes

  • Raw return: close[t+h] / close[t] − 1.
  • Neutralized return: raw return minus that day's equal-weighted basket mean. This is the forward-return analog of the Sprint 15 cross-sectional residual analysis.
  • Bin boundaries: cut at 0.50, 0.60, 0.70, 0.80, 0.90, 0.95, 1.00. The 0.60–0.80 "velocity band" matches BERYL Sprint 16's entry gate. The 0.90–1.00 range is the "structural drift" zone where the HMM has high confidence but realized returns historically disappoint.
  • Interpretation: a healthy entry gate should bin into confidence ranges where neutralized forward returns are systematically positive. Any bin with significantly negative neutralized returns is a candidate for exclusion or short-side flipping.
