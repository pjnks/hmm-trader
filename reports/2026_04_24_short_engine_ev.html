Methodology Notes

  • Neutralization: For each scan_date, subtract the equal-weighted mean forward return of all 98 tickers from each ticker's individual forward return. Equivalent to the deployed hedge: short the target asset, long an equal-weighted basket of the rest.
  • Short-side P&L: pnl = -1 × neutralized_forward_return. A profitable short occurs when the target underperforms the basket.
  • Dynamic exit: For each entry signal at date t, the exit date is the first t+k (k ∈ [1,10]) where ticker confidence ≥ 0.6. If confidence never recovers, the position holds the maximum 10-day window and reports T+10.
  • Friction asymmetry: Fixed-horizon trades pay friction proportional to the horizon. Dynamic-exit friction uses the realized average hold duration.
  • Sharpe-like proxy: per-trade mean / std × √(252 / hold_days). Annualized single-trade-equivalent — NOT a full strategy Sharpe (no path or vol-of-vol modeling).
