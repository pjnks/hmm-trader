## 1. AGATE — Crypto Multi-Ticker Rotation

### 1.1 What It Does
Scans 16 Coinbase-listed crypto derivatives every 4 hours. For each ticker:
1. Fetches ~2,200 hourly OHLCV bars from Polygon.io
2. Resamples to the ticker's optimal timeframe (3h or 4h)
3. Computes HMM features (log returns, price range, volume change, + extended features depending on config)
4. Fits an **ensemble** of 3 GaussianHMMs with n_states = [N-1, N, N+1] where N is the per-ticker optimum
5. Majority-votes on regime classification: **BULL**, **BEAR**, or **CHOP**
6. Averages posterior confidence across the 3 models
7. Runs 8 technical indicator confirmation checks (RSI, momentum, volatility, volume, ADX, price trend, MACD, stochastic)
8. Generates BUY signal if: regime=BULL AND confidence >= 0.70 AND confirmations >= threshold (per-ticker, typically 5-8)

## 2. BERYL — NDX100 Single-Stock Ensemble Rotation

### 2.1 What It Does
Scans 98 NDX100 equities daily (pre-market). For each ticker:
1. Fetches 365 days of daily OHLCV from Polygon.io (~252 trading days)
2. Fits ensemble HMM with per-ticker optimal n_states (from `beryl_per_ticker_configs.json`)
3. If ensemble fails to converge, falls back to single HMMRegimeModel(n_states=4, cov_type="diag")
4. Scores BUY signals: `score = confidence × (confirmations / threshold) × insider_boost`
5. **Insider scoring:** Fetches SEC Form 4 filings via EDGAR. Filters out routine 10b5-1 pre-planned sales. Only penalizes extreme discretionary selling (>10x buy value, 3+ unique sellers → 0.85x multiplier). Net buying → up to 1.15x boost.
6. Holds up to 3 positions ($833 each), per-ticker cooldowns after exit.
7. Intraday risk checks every 4h on all open positions.

## 5. Statistical Caveats

### 5.1 Everything Is Underpowered
- BERYL: N=1 closed trade. No statistical inference possible.
- CITRINE: N=8 closed trades. Wilson CI on win rate: [13.7%, 69.4%]. Need N≥30 for a useful confidence interval.
- AGATE: N=0 trades. Signal generation only.

### 5.2 Walk-Forward Sharpe ≠ Live Sharpe
The optimizer WF Sharpes (e.g., BCH +3.515, ETH +5.063) are the best configs found by searching a large parameter space. This is **optimizer selection bias** — you're looking at the peak of a noisy distribution. Expected live degradation: 75-90%. A WF Sharpe of +3.5 might yield live Sharpe of +0.35 to +0.88.

### 5.3 The HMM Is Not Predicting, It's Classifying
The GaussianHMM labels the current market **state** (BULL/BEAR/CHOP) based on the statistical properties of recent price action. It does not predict future returns. The trading logic assumes that regimes persist (a market currently in BULL will remain BULL for some time). This assumption is correct on average but fails at regime transitions — which is exactly where the largest losses occur (INTU -$134 on a regime flip).

### 5.4 Ensemble Voting Reduces Signal Frequency
With 3 HMMs needing to agree (majority vote), the ensemble is more conservative than a single model. AGATE had only 2-4 BUY signals per scan out of 16 tickers despite 4-7 being in BULL regime. Many BULL tickers fail the confirmation threshold. This is by design — higher precision, lower recall — but it means fewer trades and slower P&L accumulation during test mode.
